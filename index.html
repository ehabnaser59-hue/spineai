Step 1 of 3 · McKenzie MDT
Directional Preference
Perform repeated end-range movement testing. Identify the direction that most consistently reduces or centralises symptoms.
Direction of Preference · Kilpikoski et al. (2002) κ=0.69
ℹAsk patient to perform 10 repeated end-range movements in each direction. Note which direction reduces, centralises or abolishes symptoms.
Symptom Response · Werneke et al. (1999) κ=0.84
ℹCentralisation: distal symptoms moving proximally toward lumbar spine. Peripheralisation: symptoms spreading distally — contraindication to continuing that direction.

MDT Syndrome Classification · Hefford (2008) κ=0.60
ℹDerangement: clear DP + centralisation (50–78% of NSLBP). Dysfunction: end-range pain only, no DP. Postural: pain from sustained posture only. Other: does not fit mechanical patterns.
